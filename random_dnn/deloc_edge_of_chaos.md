# The delocalised edge of chaos and its finite-size structure

Results document for the composite phase diagram
(`fig/ht_mlp_jacobian/fuzzy_edge_phase_diagram.png`, generated by
`.agents/scripts/fuzzy_edge_phase_diagram.py`): the order/chaos boundary of
heavy-tailed MLPs when the susceptibility is restricted to delocalised
Jacobian modes, its estimator theory, and its finite-size (finite-width)
structure.  Derivational prerequisites: the singular-value density and
mobility edge of the layer Jacobian (`ht_mlp_jacobian.md` secs. 3, 6; the
mobility edge is the zero of the cavity growth rate of the mean log
imaginary part of the resolvent, `RMT/localisation.md`).
Conventions: alpha in (1, 2] is the weight stability index, sigma_W the
weight scale, s a singular value of the layer Jacobian J = D W, rho(s)
its N -> infinity density, s^*(alpha, sigma_W) the mobility edge.

## 1. The delocalised susceptibility

For alpha < 2 the full susceptibility (mean-square singular value)

$$\chi_1 = \int_0^\infty s^2 \rho(s)\, ds$$

diverges: the integrand tail $s^{-1-\alpha} \cdot s^2$ is not integrable.
At finite N the divergence is realised as a few localised outlier modes
carrying almost all of the $s^2$ weight.  Because a localised mode
concentrates its output on O(1) units -- saturating them and failing to
compound across layers -- the propagation-relevant object is the
**delocalised susceptibility**

$$\chi_1^{\rm deloc}(\alpha, \sigma_W)
   = \int_0^{s^*} s^2 \rho(s)\, ds,$$

with the conditional variant $\chi_1^{\rm cond} = \chi_1^{\rm deloc} /
\int_0^{s^*}\rho$ (per-channel gain of the extended sector; the two
nearly coincide at the boundary since the spectral mass below $s^*$ is
0.97-0.99 there).  The unit contour

$$\chi_1^{\rm deloc}(\alpha, \sigma_W) = 1$$

is the **delocalised edge of chaos**: above it, the extended modes alone
amplify a generic signal; below it they attenuate, and any residual
amplification is carried by non-compounding localised outliers.  It
generalises the classical edge of chaos (Poole et al., Schoenholz et
al.; `.agents/notes/poole-2016-preprint`,
`.agents/notes/schoenholz-2017-preprint`), to which it reduces at
alpha = 2 where no localised modes exist and $\chi_1$ is finite.

It sits in a hierarchy of three gain measures.  (i) The typical (geometric-mean) gain $\exp\langle\ln
s^2\rangle = \exp[(1/N)\ln\det(J^TJ)]$ is finite, self-averaging and
N-independent, but draws no line: it factorises exactly as
$2(\mathbb{E}\ln\phi'(h) + \ln\sigma_W + c_\alpha^{\det})$ (with
$c_\alpha^{\det}$ the unit Levy-matrix log-det constant, $= -1/2$ at
alpha = 2) and its maximum over the whole plane is $e^{-1}$, attained
at exactly $(\alpha, \sigma_W) = (2, 1)$ -- tanh networks are
volume-contracting everywhere; chaos is carried entirely by the top of
the spectrum.  (ii) The delocalised susceptibility above -- the
asymptotic boundary.  (iii) The raw arithmetic mean $\langle s^2
\rangle$ -- infinite in expectation for alpha < 2; its finite-N
realisation is fixed by the trace identity $\sum_i s_i^2 = \sum_{ij}
J_{ij}^2$ plus the generalized CLT: an $(\alpha/2)$-stable variable
with median $\propto \sigma_W^2 \langle D^\alpha\rangle^{2/\alpha}
N^{2/\alpha - 1}$, validated with no fitted constants (median
measured/predicted 0.98-1.03 across N = 350-2800, per-cell scatter
matching the predicted stable band).  Its unit contour is the drifting
finite-N floor of sec. 4.

Inputs: rho(s) from the Belinschi-type scalar closure
(`ht_mlp_jacobian.md` sec. 3; `.agents/notes/belinschi-2008.md`);
$s^*$ from cavity population dynamics on the linearised Im channel --
the zero of the growth rate $\varphi(s)$ of the mean log imaginary
part of the resolvent (`RMT/localisation.md`), with the doubling ladder
and Brunet-Derrida extrapolation controlling the finite-pool bias.
(The 2026-07 production npz was computed with the equivalent
frozen-moment readout $\min_m \log\lambda(m,s)/m$ of the same
recursion; the two coincide in the pool limit.)
Artifacts: `fig/ht_mlp_jacobian/phi_star_phase_diagram;...npz` (the
transition), `phi_star_truncated_chi1;...npz` (chi at all cutoffs);
recut machinery `.agents/scripts/chi1_mc_recut.py`.

## 2. The asymptotic boundary

On the production grid the delocalised edge of chaos is nearly flat and
U-shaped in alpha:

$$\sigma_W(\chi_1^{\rm deloc} = 1) \approx
  1.06\ (\alpha = 1.1)\ \to\ 0.94\ (\alpha = 1.4\text{-}1.5)\ \to\
  1.06\ (\alpha = 1.9)\ \to\ 1\ (\alpha = 2),$$

meeting the Gaussian corner continuously (top-rung cut; the
BD-extrapolated cut sits 0.05-0.15 lower; conditional variant 0.01-0.04
lower).  The contour is far better conditioned than pointwise chi_1:
rung-to-rung ladder drift moves it by only 0.02-0.05 in sigma_W because
the chi map is steep near the crossing.  The mild dip at intermediate
alpha says the delocalised bulk alone is most amplifying at moderate
tail heaviness.

The boundary is q-independent by construction: it is defined by an
s-space partition at the mobility edge, and the multifractal order q
enters only through estimators (sec. 3).

## 3. Estimator theory: D_q cuts

Mode-classification estimators truncate by the generalized dimension
D_q of singular vectors rather than by s.  Their asymptotics follow
from the amplitude statistics:

- **Extended branch:** Porter-Thomas moments give the finite-N plateau
  $D_q = 1 - \ln[(2q-1)!!] / ((q-1)\ln N)$ (e.g. $D_2 = 1 - \ln 3/\ln N
  \approx 0.83$ at N = 700).
- **Localised branch:** weights carry a power-law hierarchy with tail
  exponent beta(s), now MEASURED (Hill estimator on the stationary MC
  front): beta = 1/2 exactly at the
  edge (freezing; measured 0.496-0.50 at $s/s^* = 1.02$), rising with s
  to the hybridisation value $\alpha/2$ deep in the tail (measured
  0.57 / 0.77 / ~1.0 vs alpha/2 = 0.6 / 0.75 / 0.9 at alpha =
  1.2 / 1.5 / 1.8) -- deep in the tail a single heavy amplitude
  dominates each cavity step, so the stationary tail inherits the
  one-step exponent.  The asymptotic spectrum is the frozen/Zipf one:

  $$D_q = \frac{\beta - q}{\beta(1 - q)} > 0 \ (q < \beta), \qquad
    D_q = 0 \ (q \ge \beta).$$

  For $q \ge \alpha/2$ every localised mode has $D_q = 0$: those cuts
  jump $1 \to 0$ at $s^*$.  For $q < \alpha/2$ the deep tail retains a
  positive asymptotic dimension (non-monotone in s: ~0 just above the
  edge where beta ~ 1/2 <= q, positive again deep where beta > q) --
  heavy-tailed localisation is algebraic, never
  exponential-Anderson-like.  Crucially, the tail exponent alone does
  NOT determine finite-N estimators: real localised modes have finite
  participation volume (deep Levy outliers are few-site resonances),
  so their apparent $D_q$ at N = 700 sits far below the full-support
  Zipf value (sec. 4).

Consequences, all verified empirically (`fig/ht_mlp_jacobian/cstar.npz`,
the exact multi-q D_q-cut campaign, `.agents/scripts/cstar_grid.py`):

- For $q \ge 1/2$, any threshold $c \in (0, 1)$ asymptotically recovers
  the same partition; at finite N the estimator is biased by the
  crossover band.  Aggressive cuts (e.g. $D_2 > 0.5$) **amputate**
  below-edge weight (84/56/18% of it at alpha = 1.2/1.5/1.9) and push
  the contour up; the exact cut reproducing the boundary is
  $c^* \approx 0.03$-$0.06$ at N = 700, an order of magnitude below the
  conventional 0.5, and is not calibratable (it varies over the diagram
  and shrinks with N).
- For $q < 1/2$ the usable window narrows to (localised-branch maximum,
  extended plateau) and closes at $q \to 0$ ($D_0 = 1$ on both sides).
  Shallow cuts **contaminate**: the $q = 0.5$, $c^* = 0.1$ contour sits
  at $\sigma_W \approx 0.53$-$0.68$ because retained localised outliers,
  not extended modes, carry its unit susceptibility.
- The **spectral cut** (s-cutoff at the measured edge) implements the
  definition directly, matches the analytic truncated chi to 0.3-4%,
  and is the recommended empirical analogue.

## 4. Finite-size structure: the band

At finite N the boundary is a band, not a line
(`fuzzy_edge_phase_diagram.png`):

- **Lower bound (contamination):** the shallow-cut contour above, at
  $\sigma_W \approx 0.5$-$0.7$.  Its depth is now PREDICTED by a
  balance equation: the cut retains
  the s-window $(s^*, s_{\rm ret})$ where the apparent $D_q(s, N)$
  profile exceeds the threshold, and
  $\chi_1^{\rm deloc} + \int_{s^*}^{s_{\rm ret}} s^2\rho\,ds = 1$
  reproduces the empirical q = 0.5, c = 0.1 chi to 2-8% per cell and
  the contour to 0.00-0.05 in sigma_W (predicted 0.540/0.580/0.725 vs
  empirical 0.540/0.604/0.777 at alpha = 1.2/1.5/1.8, N = 700).  The
  density side is fully analytic; $s_{\rm ret}$ is currently measured
  (per-mode $D_q(s)$ profiles) -- its first-principles theory is the
  participation-volume profile V(s), open (sec. 6).
- **Upper bound (ergodic / plateau onset):** the arc where even
  apparently-extended modes alone carry $\chi = 1$, i.e. where the
  D_q-cut $c^*$ maps reach the extended plateau.  Empirically
  $\sigma_W = 1.29$ at alpha = 1.95 rising to 2.44 at 1.65 and past
  $\sigma_W = 3$ below alpha $\approx 1.6$ (N = 700).
- The sharp asymptotic line (sec. 2) threads between them.  Below the
  band sits the raw (untruncated) finite-N edge of chaos, where the
  bare $\langle s^2 \rangle$ of the sampled ensemble crosses 1; it has
  no asymptotic position (drifts to $\sigma_W = 0$ with N, as
  $N^{1/2 - 1/\alpha}$ from the exact stable-law floor of sec. 1) and
  marks only outlier-driven, non-compounding amplification.

The upper arc has analytic structure: it is an iso-contour of the
frozen Lyapunov exponent evaluated at the gain-unity spectral cutoff
$s_\gamma$ ($\int_0^{s_\gamma} s^2\rho = 1$):

$$\varphi^*(s_\gamma(\alpha, \sigma_W)) = \varphi_{\rm thr}, \qquad
  \varphi_{\rm thr} \approx 8,$$

constant to ~15% along the arc while $\varphi^*(s_\gamma)$ spans
$(-0.5, 8.7)$ along a single alpha column.  Critically, the N-ladder
(N = 350-2800 at fixed pooled-mode count) shows $\varphi_{\rm thr}$ is
**N-independent**: the onsets do not relax as $1/\ln^2 N$ (the
correlation-volume/Brunet-Derrida form is refuted at accessible sizes).
The band is therefore effectively frozen -- the Levy-matrix
apparent-nonergodicity crossover, ergodic only beyond astronomically
large correlation volumes -- and should be treated as a fixed feature
of any realisable system.  Between the arc and the mobility edge, the
delocalised modes carry an s-dependent but N-independent apparent D_2
deficit: an effectively multifractal delocalised regime.

Two anchors are sharp and verified: at alpha = 2 the band pinches to
the single point $\sigma_W = 1$ (onset 1.05/1.02/0.95/0.95 across the
N-ladder -- the classical edge of chaos, no localised modes, no
crossover), and the band width grows monotonically as alpha decreases
(the crossover weight fractions of sec. 3).

## 5. Reading and reporting

Recommended presentation: quote the asymptotic delocalised edge of
chaos as *the* boundary (well-defined, estimator-free, ladder-robust),
and draw the finite-N band as the physical reality for finite networks:
in (sigma_W, at fixed alpha) order, the regimes are ordered ->
outlier-driven amplification -> crossover (boundary
definition-dependent) -> ergodic chaos.  A finite heavy-tailed network
does not cross the delocalised edge of chaos at a point; it traverses a
regime whose width grows toward alpha = 1.

## 6. Open items

- What sets $\varphi_{\rm thr} \approx 8$: it is neither $\ln N$ nor
  the BD constant per se; a theory of the apparent D_2 deficit as a
  function of $\varphi^*(s)$ would fix it.
- RESOLVED (sec. 3, log 1k): the deep-tail amplitude exponent is the
  hybridisation value $\alpha/2$ (beta rises 1/2 -> alpha/2 with s;
  Hill measurement, residual pool drift ~0.1 per doubling at P = 2e4).
  Remaining quantitative gap: a BD-style extrapolation or a P = 8e4
  rung to pin the interpolating shape beta(s).
- The participation-volume profile V(s): the missing first-principles
  ingredient of the band-depth balance equation (log 1k) -- a theory of
  the apparent $D_q(s, N)$ of localised modes would replace the
  measured retention window $s_{\rm ret}$.  Candidates: cavity
  correlation volume from $v_{\rm typ}(s)$; resonance counting with the
  measured beta(s).  Corollary to check: since deep-tail
  $D_{0.5} > 0$ asymptotically, the q = 0.5 contamination contour
  should drift down at large N (rerun the $D_q(s)$ profiles at the
  nladder sizes).
- Conditional vs truncated normalisation for trainability (the aligned
  vs isotropic gain): measure
  per-layer gradient log-gain of a trained network where the two
  separate, e.g. (alpha, sigma_W) = (1.8, 2.0).
- The alpha = 2 column of the density branch (Gaussian/MP) to close the
  boundary analytically at the pinch, and the alpha = 1 column mass
  deficit of the density solver.
